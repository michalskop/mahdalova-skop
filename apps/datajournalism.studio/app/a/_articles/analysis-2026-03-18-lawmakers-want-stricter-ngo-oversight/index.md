_The governing coalition is preparing stricter rules for non-profit organization finances. But the lawmakers pushing the law somehow don't mention that similar rules already exist. And that they themselves don't follow them._

_We found that more than a third of governing coalition lawmakers directly lead some non-profit organization and that most of these organizations don't follow current laws and don't publish even the most basic financial data._

Specifically, we found **fifty-five non-profit organizations that have one of the one hundred eight coalition lawmakers directly in leadership**. Membership itself is not tracked and therefore cannot be quantified, so we focus exclusively on organization leadership. Even from this data, the quite rich associational life of our legislators is apparent.

``` box right info
#### Government Lawmakers' NGOs

- 32 violate the law, don't publish basic financial documents
- 19 comply
- 4 newly established (obligation will arise only this year)
```

Current laws clearly state that every non-profit organization (association, institute, etc.) must **publish basic accounting documents in the Collection of Documents every year**. Last year, only nineteen of the mentioned fifty-five organizations fulfilled this obligation. Thirty-two did not. Four NGOs were newly established and therefore had nothing to publish yet.

## <PartyFace party="Motoristé" size={24} text="MOTO" /> Motorists: Clean Zero
Lawmakers for **Motorists to Themselves** are doing worst. In total, they lead eight associations - and **not one of them fulfills the legal obligation**. (The exception here is only one new association that does not yet have a legal obligation to publish accounting documents.)

Minister of Foreign Affairs and party chairman [Petr Macinka](https://snemovna.datatimes.cz/member/7022) chairs the **Motorists Club** and is secretary of the board of the **Center for Terrorism Research**. Both associations violate the law — they have never submitted accounting documents to the Collection of Documents.

### Macinka's 800 Thousand from Nowhere
Moreover, the **Motorists Club** donated **[800 thousand crowns](https://www.hlidacstatu.cz/sponzori/strana/05989175?rok=2024)** to the **Motorists to Themselves** party in 2024 - whose chairman is again Macinka. Where did that money come from and what portion of the association's budget did they donate? Due to the absence of mandatory documents, this cannot be determined.

Lawmaker [Filip Turek](https://snemovna.datatimes.cz/member/7059) leads two associations with similarly ostentatious names - Jaguar Club of the Czech Republic and Aston Martin Club of the Czech Republic. **Neither publishes financial documents**.

Same situation with [Gabriela Sedláčková](https://snemovna.datatimes.cz/member/7041) (PLURALIS association), [Renata Vesecká](https://snemovna.datatimes.cz/member/7054) (Czech-Chinese Literary Publication Exchange Centre) and [Matěj Gregor](https://snemovna.datatimes.cz/member/7006) (Exit association), who collects money for Czechia's exit from the European Union, although he simultaneously declares that he [does not wish for this exit](https://denikn.cz/1866394/poslanec-za-motoristy-vybira-penize-na-vystoupeni-z-eu-pritom-je-proti/).

## Tighten Rules, or First Fulfill Your Own Basic Obligations?

``` box right info
#### NGO Obligations Today
- Every year send financial documents to the Collection of Documents (financial statements, annual report, or overview of assets and liabilities)

- Documents are publicly available in the Collection of Documents.

- Failure to comply threatens sanctions. According to the Accounting Act, a fine of up to 100 thousand crowns can be imposed and with long-term non-compliance, the organization can even be dissolved
```

The paradox is obvious: lawmakers who want to legislatively force NGOs to greater transparency **themselves ignore the law that already requires basic transparency today**. Meanwhile, large professional NGOs - People in Need, ADRA, Doctors Without Borders, charities - publish financial information beyond legal requirements. The new law would therefore add obligations to organizations that already voluntarily fulfill them, while those led by government lawmakers themselves ignore the law.

The situation is also illustrated by [Foreign Minister Macinka's statement](https://www.ceskenoviny.cz/zpravy/2800148), who declared that non-profit organizations started "shouting loudly" when the government came with a requirement to disclose financing. A minister leading two associations that **never submitted legal documents about their finances** comments on the insufficient transparency of others.

It should also be added that the government wants to achieve with the new law what should already exist today. The Ministry of Finance maintains a [Subsidy Register](https://data.mf.gov.cz/topics/dotace.html) at the state level. Other levels (regions, EU) are added by an NGO: [State Watch has long been making public subsidies transparent](https://www.hlidacstatu.cz/dotace).

## <PartyFace party="Motoristé" size={24} text="MOTO" /> Motorist Lawmakers in NGO Leadership
As we already mentioned, party chairman and minister [Petr Macinka](https://snemovna.datatimes.cz/member/7022) leads two NGOs - [Motorists Club](https://klubmotoristu.cz/) and [Center for Terrorism Research](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Bohbot#Centrum_pro_v%C3%BDzkum_terorismu), which **never submitted legal documents about their finances**.

We devoted detailed attention to two NGOs led by lawmaker [Filip Turek](https://snemovna.datatimes.cz/member/7059) in the article [MEP Turek: President of Two Associations, One Has 3 Members, the Other Doesn't Function](/clanek/kontext-2025-05-14-europoslanec-turek-prezident-dvou-spolku). Lawmaker Turek's NGOs also don't publish mandatory financial documents.

The [Exit](https://odchod.eu/) association under [Matěj Gregor](https://snemovna.datatimes.cz/member/7006)'s leadership promotes Czech Republic's withdrawal from the EU and has collected hundreds of thousands of crowns in its transparent account since its inception. Gregor himself admits that the association doesn't actually function today - but claims he won't abandon it and wants to make it a "cross-party platform" for Euroskeptic topics. Gregor himself paradoxically [does not wish for Czech Republic's exit from the EU](https://denikn.cz/1866394/poslanec-za-motoristy-vybira-penize-na-vystoupeni-z-eu-pritom-je-proti/). Again, this is an association that, contrary to law, doesn't publish its financial documents.

What activity the **Czech-Chinese Literary Publication Exchange Centre** under lawmaker [Renata Vesecká](https://snemovna.datatimes.cz/member/7054)'s leadership actually conducts cannot be publicly determined - the association has no website, no media reports on it in any way. Since its founding in 2017, the association has not published any financial documents.

The [Pluralis](https://www.pluralis.cz) association, where [Gabriela Sedláčková](https://snemovna.datatimes.cz/member/7041) is vice-chair, promotes conservative ideology for the LGBT+ community. But it also doesn't publish its financial documents.

## <PartyFace party="ANO" size={24} text="ANO" /> ANO Lawmakers in NGO Leadership

A special case is the **Doctors Against Wars** association — it has two government lawmakers in leadership, [Ivana Mádlová](https://snemovna.datatimes.cz/member/6732) and [Věra Adámková](https://snemovna.datatimes.cz/member/6254) (both ANO). This association also doesn't fulfill legal obligations. Doctor Věra Adámková additionally leads two other associations, the Světice Hunting Association and the Healthy Heart Foundation, both also without mandatory financial documents.

Lawmakers [Marek Novák](https://snemovna.datatimes.cz/member/6533) and [Denis Doksanský](https://snemovna.datatimes.cz/member/7000) lead the most NGOs - both four organizations.

[Marek Novák](https://snemovna.datatimes.cz/member/6533), besides two football associations, also leads the **[Czech-Tunisian Chamber of Commerce for Mutual Cooperation](https://czetun.com/index.html)** and the **[Czech-Austrian Chamber of Commerce and Industry](https://austriaczech.eu/)**. But neither has published mandatory financial documents.

Lawmaker [Denis Doksanský](https://snemovna.datatimes.cz/member/7000) leads NGOs connected with Sion schools ([Sion High School](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sion_High_School)) - the Sion association (school operator), Sion Christian Center, Sion - New Generation association and sports club [FBC Sion - Hradec Lions](https://www.fbcsion.cz/). They rather publish financial documents, but most are missing for the last year 2024.

Among other associations led by ANO lawmakers we find, for example, the **Czech Hemp Cluster**, led by [Václav Trojan](https://snemovna.datatimes.cz/member/7053), a research worker specializing in medical cannabis. This association publicly engages in cannabis legislation. The association publishes documents, only the last year 2024 is missing.

[Martin Kolovratník](https://snemovna.datatimes.cz/member/6211), chairman of the parliamentary electoral committee, leads the **Association for Transport Development**, whose website records dozens of trips and lobbying meetings with companies from the transport sector. They published documents, but year 2024 is again missing.
