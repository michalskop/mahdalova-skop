Political parties' answers to 100 key questions reveal where the Czech Republic is heading. Based on **100 questions from the [Voting Calculator](https://www.volebnikalkulacka.cz/), we bring you a unique overview** of where the parties of the planned coalition agree, where they diverge, and on which topics they will need to seek compromises or support across the Parliament.

Some positions unite everyone. For example, support for housing construction goes across the political spectrum. Similarly, nuclear energy has exceptionally strong support in Czechia – and there is 100% agreement among parliamentary parties on this issue, just as with the construction of additional dams. And finally, in the case of nuclear energy, there is 100% agreement that Czech legislators will continue to support it.

## Where the Planned Government Disagrees
<PartyFace party="ANO" size={15} text="" />  <PartyFace party="SPD" size={15} text="" /> <PartyFace party="Motoristé" size={15} text="" /> It still holds that ANO plans to govern together with SPD and Motoristé. Let's go through the questions where these parties disagree. Among themselves, but often also with no one else.

### Alone Against All
<PartyFace party="ANO" size={15} text="" /> An exemplary case is when ANO is the only party in the entire Parliament that wants to restore EET (electronic sales records).

<PartyFace party="SPD" size={15} text="" /> SPD, on the other hand, is isolated in wanting to move to neutrality regarding the war in Ukraine. And they want to leave the EU.

<PartyFace party="Motoristé" size={15} text="" /> Motoristé are the only ones who want tuition fees to be charged at least at some public universities. Neither ANO nor SPD want tuition fees, and the entire opposition agrees with them on this.

### SPD and Motoristé Against Others
<PartyFace party="SPD" size={15} text="" /> <PartyFace party="Motoristé" size={15} text="" /> SPD and Motoristé would abolish the recently introduced postal voting. In energy, they would not want to support renewable sources and do not want support for reducing the energy intensity of buildings.

Similarly, they do not want the police to take more decisive action against polluting cars with non-functional filters.

## Government vs. Opposition
<PartyFace party="ANO" size={15} text="" />  <PartyFace party="SPD" size={15} text="" /> <PartyFace party="Motoristé" size={15} text="" /> If a government of ANO, SPD, and Motoristé is indeed formed, then the governing parties would have a majority over the opposition on issues such as:
- Not adopting the euro
- Fossil fuels as the main source of energy in the Czech Republic
- Propagation of communism should not be a criminal offense
- Withdrawal from the migration pact

## Complex Coalitions
On many questions, there is no agreement between the parties of the forming government, nor between the parties that today appear to end up in opposition.

<PartyFace party="ANO" size={15} text="" /> <PartyFace party="STAN" size={15} text="" /> <PartyFace party="Piráti" size={15} text="" /> For example, euthanasia in the Czech Republic is supported by a majority composed of ANO, STAN, and Piráti.

<PartyFace party="ANO" size={15} text="" /> <PartyFace party="Piráti" size={15} text="" /> <PartyFace party="SPD" size={15} text="" /> A general referendum would have a majority composed of ANO, Piráti, and SPD.
